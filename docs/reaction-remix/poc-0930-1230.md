keeping the source reaction material intact. Three commentary intervals inside

## 3. Technical Findings

- Commentary and reaction speech were sufficiently separated in the tested
  range to replace narration without materially cutting participant audio.
- The selected AI33 voice produced intelligible Japanese in the test.
- Preserving original reaction audio while inserting new commentary requires an
  audio-aware timeline; the existing recap renderer's video-only EDL and muted
  source-audio behavior are not an appropriate production contract.
- Source music/ambience can help preserve the channel's existing sound, but it
  must be separated or ducked carefully so the old narrator does not leak under
  the new commentary.
- Script revision to fit a fixed editorial window sounded better than aggressive
  time-stretching.
- Boundary fades and loudness normalization are necessary even when the visual
  frames are left untouched.

## 4. Superseded Visual Experiment

An early POC variant masked old burned-in captions and drew replacement
subtitles. That direction is explicitly rejected by the final product scope.

The approved behavior is:

- Do not mask, blur, or cover existing burned-in subtitles.
- Do not add new subtitles.
- Do not alter mascot, logo, branding, or reaction frames.
- Preserve the source image during retained reaction clips.

Any visual-mask or subtitle-generation artifacts from the exploratory POC are
not requirements and must not be carried into implementation.

## 5. What the POC Did Not Prove

- It did not analyze or remix the complete 18:49 video.
- It did not reorder reaction units into a new editorial arc.
- It did not validate automatic multilingual speaker-role classification.
- It did not enforce the final 10–20% duration-reduction policy.
- It did not provide a production JSON contract, cache strategy, CLI, or
  orchestrator integration.
- It did not prove that every source commentary interval can be isolated without
  reaction overlap.

These gaps are the focus of the planned full-video reaction-remix pipeline.

## 6. Decisions Locked After the POC

- Full-video re-editing is allowed and preferred over fixed 1:1 commentary
  replacement.
- Reaction units may be reordered to improve hook, progression, climax, and
  ending.
- Retained reaction picture, original audio, playback speed, and burned-in
  subtitles must remain intact.
- Only Japanese editorial commentary is rewritten and synthesized.
- AI33 voice ID remains `elevenlabs_QPtBgsg1dxKTQHNpHrHt`.
- The normal output target is 85–90% of source duration. The permitted range is
  80–100%, with 80% as a hard automatic floor.
- No subtitle masking or replacement subtitle layer is permitted.

## 7. Production Follow-Up

Before implementation, the design still needs to lock:

- Namespaced JSON contracts for source analysis, editorial plan, commentary,
  audio-aware timeline, and QA.
- Safe handling of `mixed` intervals where narrator and reaction overlap.
- Full-video story planning and duration-budget allocation.
- Original audio, music-bed, narrator-removal, and crossfade policies.
- Automated checks for reaction completeness, old-narrator leakage, A/V sync,
  duration floor, loudness, decode integrity, and reorder continuity.
